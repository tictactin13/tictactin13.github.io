Learn More About Me

I'm a front-end developer from Omagh, Northern Ireland; striving to create beautiful, responsive websites and striking brand identities for small businesses and local clients.

Concept

Every good design starts with a clear concept. First, I collaborate with the client to recognise the mission, the content, the demographic, and then generate a clear design direction for the project.

Create

Creating wireframes, visualising use cases, I figure out the best structure based on the content. I design layouts, get the client's opinion, and iterate until a final visual design has been completed.

Code

Using HTML5, CSS3, and Javascript, I convert the final ideas to a full functioning site making sure to test on different devices and browsers to make certain that all elements are pixel perfect on every screen.

Question and Answer

I mean, sure, beans on toast is arguably top 5 snack material, but why name your site after it?

First of all, great question. Second of all, my site is actually beansANDtoast not beansONtoast. I named it after the three things that accompany my mornings when I code: beans as in coffee, Beans as in my cat, and a couple slices of toast (or sometimes five).

That's cool and all, but I still don't see why you decided to name your portfolio site that.

Wow, that's not even a question. And also, I see your point but by the time I realised I should've just called it justinjesalva.com my trial period was already over and it was too late to get a refund for this one. And I'm not about to pay for two domains when I only need one. That would just show bad business acumen.
