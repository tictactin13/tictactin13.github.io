Read Case Study

ECC is a newly founded church that needed help with their logo and branding.

Design Process

Preconceived Idea

I actually started this project with an initial idea that was pretty much completed. Having seen logos of this style on various logo design websites, I wanted to jump on the bandwagon myself as the style seemed to be gaining popularity.


The clients didn’t like the idea though and I’m glad they pushed for a different direction (though I wasn't at the time) as the final design that came about is better in every way (in my opinion) and less reliant on what may very well be a seasonal trend.


Sketches

After much deliberation, the client and I settled on a design with a tree as the focal point. Trees bring about a connotation of strength, longitude, wisdom etc. The following sketches are just a few of the loose ideas I came up with.



Typography

The strong contrast between thick and thin strokes on Didiot pairs well with the uniform thin lines of Helvetica Neue Light.


Old Oak Tree

In my mind, an Oak tree personified (tree-ified?) the values I envisioned for the design, but surprisingly, oak trees are barely mentioned in the Bible and when they are mentioned, it's almost always in passing. I wanted the focal point of the logo to be something you'd recognise from a Bible story so this design was a no go.


Burning Bush

I'm almost ashamed that I didn't connect the dots sooner. One of the most well known stories in the Bible is Moses finding the flaming bush. With my focal point worked out, it was just a matter of making sure the design was recognisable and worked well with the typography. I was very happy with the design I ended up with and by designing the tree trunk after a cross, I managed to pack in a little more symbology to the logo.


Colour

Final Design

Overall, I am happy with how the design turned out. It looks quite regal and pleasing to the eye. The colours complement each other well and results in a design that is understated while still being eye catching.


It was a good experience undertaking this project, and I feel that everything I learnt from this I will definitely put to good use on future branding projects.
